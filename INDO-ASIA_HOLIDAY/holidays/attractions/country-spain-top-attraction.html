    Top Tourist Places and Attractions in SPAIN

    Plaza de Toros

    Discover the site of one of the Spain’s first Bullfighting museums! The 18th century grandeur inspired by the Colosseum is a major attraction in Valencia. The trip to Spain will be incomplete without a visit to this picture perfect work of art.

       

    Plaza de la Reina

    Plaza de la Reina is one of the oldest and busiest plazas in Valencia and the city’s beating heart. It is situated in the beautiful Old Town and marks the Kilometer 0. The center of the plaza plays host to a small patch filled with flowers and benches, where visitors can take a break and watch the world go by. The square is lined with an excellent selection of cafes, terraces, and restaurants, including one of the oldest (200 years old) and most renowned cafeterias in Valencia ...Read More

       

    Torres de Serranos

    Admire the largest gothic city gateway of Europe! The enchanting Torres de Serranos is one of must visit landmarks of Valencia. The excellent example of Gothic architecture was built in 14th century as one of the gates of the ancient city wall. Their awe-inspiring vastness and the elegance of Gothic style are truly worth your time.

       

    Torre del Oro

    Torre Del Ore also known as the Gold Tower is one of the most symbolic spots in Spain. The former watchtower holds an intriguing past that will be exciting to explore. The spiral staircase that leads to the top of the tower offers the view of Triana neighbourhood across the river that is truly enticing!

       

    Toledo Bridge

    Admire the charming Toledo Stone Bridge across the calm Manzanares River built in a Baroque style in the 17th century. The soothing reflection of the bridge on the crystal clear waters is said to be one of the best views of the Madrid City!

       

    Seville Cathedral

    How about admiring the world’s largest Gothic cathedral and the third largest church in the world? This incredible 15th century cathedral is lavishly decorated and is a veritable treasure trove of art, with notable works by Zurbaran, Murillo, Goya and others. The art collection here is breathtaking. This grandeur in the city of Seville cannot be missed!

       

    Sagrada Familia

    For centuries Montjuic served as the Barcelona’s defence hub, now a natural hill, is home to numerous Barcelona’s amazing museums, including the Miro Foundation and Poble Espanyol, a full-size Spanish model village, home to Olympic Stadium and Montjuc Palace, and some of the best panoramic view of Barcelona. The cable car (tickets available on site) will take you up the hill to get a bird’s eye view of beaches, parks and gardens of the city! This scenic hill is one of the unique spots to explore in the city. ...Read More

       

    Royal Palace

    The Spanish Royalty resided here in the 18th century but now it is used for state ceremonies. The colossal building of grey granite limestone owes its impressive appearance to amazing location! The Italian classical style palace will mesmerise you at every step, the interiors of the palace is dominated by wonderful work out art and exquisite furnishings. The largest building in Madrid is one eye-catching monument that cannot be missed!

       

    Real Alcazar

    The royal Alcazar is one marvel of Spain to explore! It is one of the oldest palaces still in use in the world. Palace’s splendid architecture, a perfect blend of Christian and Mudejar architecture, and intriguing history to it makes it more fascinating! It’s one of the most loved spots in Seville as it was featured as a location for the Game of Thrones TV series. Enough reasons to put it on your list of must visit places.

       

    Puerta del Sol

    The Puerta del Sol is a public square in Madrid, one of the best known and busiest places in the city. This is the centre of the radial network of Spanish roads. The square also contains the famous clock whose bells mark the traditional eating of the Twelve Grapes and the beginning of a new year. The New Year's celebration has been broadcast live on national television since 31 December 1962.

       

    Puerta de Alcala

    The enchanting Neo-classical monument was once one of the five doors to enter the walled city of Madrid. Situated next to the famous Parque Del Buen Retiro, the Puerta De Alcalá is an iconic monument in Madrid today, though the city is no longer walled and stretches far beyond the gate’s location but it’s worth a visit. Exploring this Roman triumphal arch featuring number of decorative elements with groups of sculptures, capitals, reliefs and masks will surely leave you in awe. ...Read More

       

    Puente de Triana

    The Puente de Isabel II, Puente de Triana or Triana Bridge, is a metal arch bridge in Seville, Spain, that connects the Triana neighbourhood with the centre of the city.

       

    Plaza Mayor

    The Plaza Mayor was first built during Philip III's reign and is a central plaza in the city of Madrid, Spain. It is located only a few Spanish blocks away from another famous plaza, the Puerta del Sol.

       

    Montjuic

    Montjuic is a prominent hill overlooking the Barcelona harbour. For ages, it played a strategic part in the defense of the city and it's one of the city's natural elevations.

       

    Granada Cathedral

    Granada Cathedral, or the Cathedral of the Incarnation is a Roman Catholic church in the city of Granada, capital of the province of the same name in the Autonomous Region of Andalusia, Spain. The cathedral is the seat of the Archdiocese of Granada.

       

    Flamenco Dance Show

    Seville is the city for you if you want to enjoy the traditional Flamenco Dance show in Spain. Dancing, singing and guitars, this intense, passionate, soulful and beautiful show is a must watch when in Seville!

       

    Cibeles Fountain

    The monumental Cibeles Fountain has become a Madrid icon. Located in the heart of the city , it shows Cybele, the Greek goddess of fertility and nature holding a sceptre and a key while being pulled by two lions on a chariot. The pull of the wild lions symbolize the power of nature or of the goddess.

       

    Buen Retiro Park

    The Buen Retiro Park is one of the largest parks of the city of Madrid, Spain. The park belonged to the Spanish Monarchy until the late 19th century, when it became a public park.

       

    Barcelona Cathedral

    Barcelona Cathedral gives you a chance to admire the perfect example of Catalan Gothic architecture from the 15th century. One of the city’s emblematic landmarks, the ancient cathedral is located in the Gothic Quarters, the historic centre of Barcelona. This Spanish marvel is a must visit when in Spain!

       

    Arc of Elvira Granada

    The Gate of Elvira is an arch located in Granada, Spain. It was declared Bien de Interés Cultural in 1896. Built in the 11th century, this Arabic gate & wall once marked the main entrance to the city.

       

    Prado Museum

    Explore one of the world’s finest collections of European art at Prado Museum. It has a diverse art collection dating back from 12th century, the largest gallery in the world is one of the must visit places in Madrid without question! It is said that the Spanish Queen was so impressed by the Louvre in Paris that she wanted to have an enormous collection in her own country and there it was, the museum has dazzling display of works by the great European masters such as Velázquez, Goya, Raphael, Rubens, and Bosch. The marvellous museum is worth the time! ...Read More

       

    City of Arts and Sciences

    If you are in Valencia, the City of Arts and Sciences cannot be missed! One of the largest scientific and cultural complexes in Europe, the complex features ballet and art to underwater world and many more curious projects that are worth exploring. This ultra-modern complex has become one of the favourite spots in Spain for tourist as well as locals. The City of Arts and Sciences will surely keep you entertained, explore it!
