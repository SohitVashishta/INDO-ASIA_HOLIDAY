    Top Tourist Places and Attractions in SOUTH AFRICA

    Hot Air Ballooning

    The famous Sun City is known for fun and adventurous activities, and Hot Ballooning is one such experience that will sweep you off your feet. Experience South Africa from above on a balloon flight over the sun-kissed plains of Pilanesberg National Park near Sun City.

       

    Cango Wildlife Ranch

    Delight yourself with the wild encounters of the Cango Wildlife Ranch. With a unique vantage point ever, Cango Wildlife Ranch offers a crocodile cage dive that allows thrill-seekers to be submerged underwater with a few giant Nile Crocodiles and observe them at the best. Apart from this exciting activity, Cango Wildlife Ranch has a variety of exhilarating activities that promises you a once in a lifetime experience.

       

    Table Mountain Aerial Cableway

    Take in the beauty of the breath-taking panorama of South African landscape with Table Mountain Aerial Cableway. Travel up Table Mountain summit at 3,501 feet and explore the unique cable car experience. The mountain gets its name from its table shaped top. A World Heritage Site and National Park and one of the new seven wonders, the table mountain is older than the Andes, the Rocky Mountain and the Himalayas. To enjoy the stunning vistas of Cape Town, head for an adventurous Table Mountain Aerial Cableway. ...Read More

       

    Sun City

    Sun City has something for everyone, with its superb resort offering attractions and world-class events. With a plethora of fun activities, Sun City is the best place to spend a beach day at the palm-fringed shore of Valley of Waves or explore the Maze of the Lost City, the largest permanent maze in the Southern Hemisphere. Spend a day at Sun City for the adrenaline-filled day!

       

    Pilanesberg Game Reserve

    One of the largest parks in South Africa, Pilanesberg Game Reserve is located on the border of entertainment complex Sun City in the North West Province. The alluring landscape circling a lake, hosts just about every mammal of southern Africa, including the famous Big Five (Rhino, Lion, Elephant, Leopard and Cape buffalo). With an abundance of flora and fauna, Pilanesberg Game Reserve is a perfect place to explore South Africa’s wildlife.

       

    Kruger National Park

    South Africa is considered to be one of the best places in the world to spot wildlife at its best. Kruger National Park is famous for its abundance of Big 5 (lion, elephant, buffalo, rhinoceros, and leopard). Also part of a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve, the national park is the perfect spot for a luxury break amidst the wildlife. Take a journey of a lifetime and explore the spectacular African wildlife of Kruger National Park.

       

    Kruger Game Drive

    Kruger Game Drive offers you a chance to get close to African wildlife and encounter the Big 5 (Elephant, Leopard, Buffalo, Rhino and Lion) of the national park. Kruger Game Drives are one of the best ways to explore the park with an experienced ranger.

       

    Knysna Lagoon Cruise

    Known for its unforgettable experience, Knysna Lagoon Cruise lets you enjoy the views of Garden Route National Park and the picture-perfect town of Knysna. Soak up the stunning vistas of the sunset as you embark on this unique experience of cruising through the fresh river waters flowing in from the surrounding Outeniqua Mountains and merging into the sea water of Indian Ocean. Catch a glimpse of world-famous Knysna Heads, marine life, and birds of South Africa. ...Read More

       

    Gold Reef City

    Settled in central Johannesburg around an authentic 19th-century gold mine, Gold Reef City is a thrilling theme park with exciting rides and historical exhibits. This family-friendly adventure park is one of the most visited places in Johannesburg that takes you back to the golden days of the country. With some of South Africa’s fastest, biggest, most twisting and turning rides, Gold Reef City is surely an adventure hub that you can’t miss. ...Read More

       

    Clifton

    A lavish suburb of Cape Town, Clifton is a coastal gem and is made up of 4 beaches that are most famous beaches of the city. Separated by a stretch of granite boulders, the beaches are sprawling and clean, reflecting the brilliant African sunshine off white sand and crystal-clear waters. To get the best of the views, one can head to one of the trendy bars or café and enjoy the spectacular vistas of the ocean.

       

    Cape of Good Hope Nature Reserve

    Situated on the Southern tip of Peninsula, Cape of Good Hope Nature Reserve is a great place for hiking, cycling bird watching, surfing and fishing. A top-notch destination in South Africa, the reserve is rich in the species of animals and birds like Oystercatcher Cormorant Ostriches and the rare Afrikaans Coastal land Penguins. The animals found here include Leopard, Spotted Hyenas, Black-Backed Jackal and mountain Zebra. Cape of Good Hope Nature Reserve is home to one of the most famous tourist attraction in Africa, Cape Point, well-known for its incredible sunset and sunrise views. Visit the Westernmost point at the reserve and take in the beauty of serene and crystal-clear waters at the beach. A part of Table Mountain National Park that stretches from Signal Hill in the north all the way to Cape Point in the South, Cape of Good Hope Nature Reserve is a must-visit in South Africa. ...Read More

       

    Bo-Kaap

    Cape Town’s little treasure, Bo Kaap, also known as the Malay Quarter, a vibrantly painted town dipped in history and unique culture tucked away under the slopes of Signal Hill. Take a stroll through the colourful houses painted with bright shades of pink, blue, yellow and orange in Bo Kaap neighbourhood which can be spotted from a mile away. The intriguing museum at Bo Kaap’s Wale Street is a perfect place to delve into the history Cape Malay Community. ...Read More

       

    Ostrich Farm

    Ostrich Farm in Oudtshoorn has a boasting number of ostriches in the world. Take a walk around the field at Ostrich Farm, hand-feed the birds, view the incubators where the eggs are hatched, and even stand on ostrich eggs. It’s an experience to visit a working ostrich farm and observe large flocks of Ostriches at different stages of development.

       

    Cango Caves

    At a distance of 29 km from Oudtshoorn lies the spectacular Cango Valley with an unbelievable underground wonder of the Cango Caves situated in Precambrian limestone at the foothills of the Swartberg Range. Explore the finest dripstone caverns that open into vast halls of towering stalagmite formations with strange names like ‘glass flower fantasy’, “weird Cango candle’ and ‘the hanging shawl’. The cave tunnel system is one of the most visited spots in South Africa and this underground wonderland is hard to miss when in Oudtshoorn, South Africa. ...Read More

       

    Boulders Beach

    Located in Simon’s Town, Boulders Bay Beach is home to over two thousand endangered African Penguins, fondly known as the Jackass Penguins, and is visited by millions of travellers to see the penguins in their natural habitat. If you want to spend your entire day with these adorable and lovely creatures, head to Boulders Beach to have an experience of a lifetime!

       

    Flying Dutchman Funicular

    Located at Cape Point, Flying Dutchman Funicular, also known as the Cape Point Funicular is a funicular railway and an exciting way to explore the spots of Cape Town. One of the easiest and quick way to reach just below the summit of Cape Point, Flying Dutchman Funicular is South Africa’s most spectacular sightseeing spot to witness the breath-taking views of the Atlantic Ocean. Visit during sunset or sunrise when the landscapes turn into a surreal vista. One of the first of its kind in South Africa, runs from the lower station at the Cape Point car park, up to an incline through dense vegetation to the upper lighthouse. ...Read More
