    Things to do in Malaysia

     

    Date : 27th January 2019 Posted By : Deveshi Share To :    

    Travel may mean different things to different people. For some, it may mean soaking up the sun on a beautiful white sand beach, and for the others, it may mean getting to know the culture and history of the destination. Funny thing is, Malaysia fits into any and every meaning of travel you have in mind. Delicious food, fascinating past, urban metropolitan architecture, white-sand beaches like nowhere else, a lot of wildlife and nature, and the cherry on the top, bag full of adventure, are things that make Malaysia what it is.

    You can snorkel around the Tioman Islands, or visit the mesmerizing Twin/Petronas Towers. Trek through tea plantations or bungee jump at Sunway Extreme Park. Penang is undoubtedly Malaysia’s food capital, considering the dishes cooked here have made it to “CNN’s top 10 must-try meals in the world”. What makes this food hub so special is the fact that most places over here have been running for decades now under the same skilled hands. It’s a paradise for all the foodies. You’ll be able to taste some of the most delicious curries, Malay dishes with international twists, Assam laksa, and so much more. Trust me, you’ll be able to find your tandoori chicken and naan as well!

    Popularly known as the “City of Entertainment”, the Genting Highlands is a great tourist attraction. Being situated 1760 meters above sea level means you’ll be able to capture the beautiful view of the valley where Kuala Lumpur lies. Once you're here, you'll forget what being bored feels like because you have hotels, amusement parks, casinos, and sky cable cars. It's safe to say that this place is the Las Vegas of Malaysia, considering the casinos are open 24x7.

    If you get bored by Malaysia (which is very unlikely to happen), you can head to Langkawi or the Perhentian Islands, two famous and touristy island paradises where you’ll be able to find some of the most stunning beaches. A little less touristy and an offbeat destination you can get to is Redang, situated on Malaysia’s east coast. It’s the perfect place to relax and to swim with baby turtles!

    A cherry on the top is the fact that Malaysia has great weather all round the year, and is an affordable destination to explore, not to forget that the locals are super helpful and kind. As I said earlier, Malaysia surely has it all!
