    How to Plan For the Long Weekends in 2020

     

    Date : 31st January 2020 Posted By : Shalu Prajesh Share To :    

    Every year we get some long weekends to plan short trips, but do we manage to use them all? I have my doubts about that. Most of the time, we fail to utilize the opportunities due to a lack of awareness and proper planning. As far as holidays are concerned, we should always plan ahead to avoid the hazards of the three budget, booking and being on time. If you're a frequent traveler, you'll know that failing to plan properly for these three things will reduce your chances of enjoying a hassle-free trip experience.

    So this year, we thought of helping people with wanderlust get a prior idea about the long weekends. As January is practically over, we are going to start with February 2020.

    Here is a list of travel opportunities with some amazing destinations to use them effectively.

    February & March 2020

    • Weekend 1  21st February (Friday MahaShivratri), 22nd(Saturday) and 23rd February (Sunday)
    • Weekend 2 7th March (Saturday), 8th March (Sunday), 9th March (Monday  take a leave), 10th March (Tuesday Holi)

    Places to Visit: Domestic and International

    • Nainital (Uttarakhand)  Boating, sailing, exploring old temples, taking the ropeway to reach small peaks, visiting the bird sanctuary, and camping - you can enjoy a plethora of activities here.
    • Varanasi (Uttar Pradesh)  Formerly known as Benares, Varanasi is one of the most important religious hubs in India, a beloved destination for pilgrims all over the world and a delight to a food-lovers sense.
    • Dubai (UAE) This is a place that you can visit any time for a short and luxurious trip. The impressive infrastructure and tall buildings amidst the golden sand dunes will take your breath away.
    • Bali (Indonesia) An equally romantic and family getaway destination, Bali offers adventure, relaxation, and mouth-watering cuisines wrapped in a single package. As there are so many things to see and take part in, contact an international tour operator in India to plan your itinerary.


    April and May 2020

    • Weekend 3  4th April (Saturday), 5th April (Sunday), 6th April (Monday Mahavir Jayanti)
    • Weekend 4  10th April (Friday Good Friday), 11th April (Saturday), 12th April (Sunday), 13th April (Monday take a leave), 14th April (Tuesday Ambedkar Jayanti)
    • Weekend 5  23rd May (Saturday), 24th May (Sunday), 25th May (Monday Eid-ul-Fitr)

    Places to Visit: Domestic

    • Mahabaleshwar (Maharashtra) Mother Nature wraps this small town in the Satara District with lush green hills, waterfalls, parks, heritage sites, and pilgrimages for the travelers to have the experience of their lives.
    • Sanchi (Madhya Pradesh)  Are you in for historical sites and Stupas? Then this is the perfect weekend getaway destination for you. Get in touch with a reputable domestic tour operator in India for an exhilarating experience.

    Places to Visit: International

    • Bhutan A Himalayan kingdom, Bhutan will fill your mind and soul with its enchanting beauty, mesmerizing views, and warm Buddhist culture.
    • Malaysia  The magnificent architecture, luxurious towns, widespread tea plantations, and the natural beauty of Malaysia are enough to create everlasting memories.


    August & September 2020

    •  Weekend 6 8th August (Saturday), 9th August (Sunday), 10th August (Monday  take a leave), 11th August (Tuesday Janmashtami)
    •  Weekend 7 31st August (Friday - Bakrid/Eid-al-Adha), 1st September (Saturday), 2nd September (Sunday)

    Places to Visit: Domestic

    • Meghalaya One of the seven sisters in the North-East, the state of lush green hills, waterfalls, and virgin forests is the best place to enjoy the monsoons.
    • Lake Gurudongmar (Sikkim)  A sacred lake, situated at a height of over 5000 meters in the lap of Himalayas, Lake Gurudongmar is a mesmerizing sight for many tourists, who travel across the country to get one glimpse of it.

    Places to Visit: International

    • Turkey An ancient country with a rich cultural history, Turkey offers a plethora of amazing sights and activities to the tourists. Start with enjoying the sacred Sufi dance of Dervishes and trying Turkish tobacco to exploring the remains of Troy and hiking to world heritage sites e.g. Nemrut Dag National Park.
    • Thailand From beach activities to nature watching and exploring the architectural marvels, this tourist hot spot in Southeast Asia has it all to turn your short weekend getaway into the experience of a lifetime. Book one of the cost-effective international holiday packages to make the most of your investment.


    October 2020

    • Weekend 8 2nd October (Friday Gandhi Jayanti), 3rd October (Saturday), 4th October (Sunday)
    • Weekend 9 23rd October (Friday Durgashtami), 24th October (Saturday), 25th October (Sunday/Dussehra)

    Places to Visit: Domestic

    • Gangtok (Sikkim) Enjoy a casual stroll down the Mall Road, visit Lake Tsomgo&Nathula Pass and explore one of the famous monasteries in Rumtek to spend a memorable weekend in the festive season.
    • Vizag (Andhra Pradesh) If you love beaches, this is one of the best places you can choose to be during fall. Apart from enjoying beach activities and collecting seashells, you can also explore the Eastern Ghats and visit ancient volcanic sites.
    • Goa, Needless to say, this is a beach paradise for all. Take part in enthralling activities like scuba diving, adventure water sports, boat cruises, and more to create unforgettable memories. Book from the list of domestic holiday packages available over the internet to cut down the costs of travel and enhance the trip experience. 

    Places to Visit: International

    • Hong Kong Wish to take a budget trip to a developed foreign country? Hong Kong offers several pocket-friendly places to stay at and inexpensive local markets to have food. You can hike along the Dragons Back trail, visit the Hong Kong Museum of Art, and take short day trips to Stanley Market, Lantau Island, and Sai Kung.
    • Vietnam Another budget international tourist destination, you'll get to experience the old Asian charm in Ho Chi Minh City, explore the Phong Nha-Ke Bang National Park and enjoy the breathtaking views of the Sapa countryside.

    November & December 2020

    • Weekend 10 14th November (Saturday/Diwali), 15th November (Sunday), 16th November (Monday  Bhai Dooj)
    • Weekend 11 28th November (Saturday), 29th November (Sunday), 30th November (Monday Guru Nanak Jayanti)
    • Weekend 12  25th December (Friday  Christmas), 26th December (Saturday), 27th December (Sunday)
    • Weekend 13 31st December (Thursday take a leave), 1st January 2021 (New Year Day), 2nd January 2021 (Saturday), 3rd January 2021 (Sunday)

    Places to Visit: Domestic

    • Ooty (Tamil Nadu) Popularly known as the Queen of the Hills, Ooty is a travelers paradise with its blue mountains, picturesque views, and the exotic beauty of nature. Nestled in the Nilgiri Hills, Ooty offers a favorable climate to travelers all across the country. Whether you wish to have a rejuvenating holiday or a second honeymoon, this place will leave no stone unturned to ensure a memorable experience.
    • Bundi (Rajasthan) A place that has witnessed historic battles and legendary warfare in the past, waits silently to enchant you with its stories of valor. If you are looking forward to experiencing the marvels of a royal trip, Bundi will not let you down.
    • New Delhi  The best place to visit the Capital City is November December. You can walk your way to the Raj Ghat memorial, where Mahatma Gandhi rests in peace, visit the historic Red Fort, where the Mughal rulers reigned supreme, and take pictures in front of the India Gate. Don't forget to visit the nearby city of Agra and see the mesmerizing Taj Mahal, as your trip will be incomplete without it.

    Places to Visit: International

    • Kenya Dreaming about the exotic tropical wildlife and natural beauty of Africa? Start with Kenya, one of the most affordable destinations in Africa to enjoy forest safaris, explore national parks, and experience the marvels of museums. Ranging from the Maasai Mara National Park to the Ngulia Rhino Sanctuary, you can fill your long days and short nights with exciting adventures.
    • Sri Lanka Christmas is the best time to visit our neighbor as the festive season is celebrated with much grandeur, offering the tourists an unforgettable experience. You can take part in Christmas feasts, shop to your heart's extent, experience amazing Midnight Mass service at the St. Lucia's Cathedral in Colombo, kite surf along the coasts of Sri Lanka and go for camping in the reserved forests and national parks to make the most of your short trip.
    • Myanmar Formerly known as Burma, Myanmar is a rich cultural heritage site, offering a budget-friendly international weekend getaway destination to Indian tourists. The place is so beautiful that you'll want to visit it again and again. Visit the Buddhist temples at Bagan, taste wine at a boutique winery, explore the Buddhist caves, and enjoy the fire-balloon festival in November to feel the cultural richness of Myanmar.

    Final words

    Traveling throughout the year may seem inconvenient to everyone but taking short trips to the aforementioned places instead of sitting at home during the long weekend breaks will surely make a difference in your life. As the year unfolds, take the reins in your hands and live as if this is the last year of your life.

    Get, set, and go!
