    Top Tourist Places and Attractions in FINLAND

    Turku Port

    Turku and the port share common history, present and future. It is difficult to think of Turku without a port, which still serves as Finland's gateway to the west, as well as for trade, tourism and culture. As part of the true Finnish maritime cluster, the port of Turku is a major source of vitality throughout the Turku region.

       

    Railway Square

    An open square by the Helsinki Railway Station, the Railway Square is surrounded by prominent buildings of the city like Ateneum - The Finnish Nation Gallery, the Finnish National Theatre and Casino Helsinki. The square also serves as a location for many events during winters.

       

    Pub Crawl Helsinki

    Discover the best of Helsinki’s drinking and the partying scene on an exciting pub crawl. To indulge in a pub crawl or bar-hopping, Kallio – a trendy and diverse district, is the best place to be. Loved by locals, Kallio is known for its karaoke experience and lively nightclubs. If you are looking for brewed beer, then head to local bars of Helsinki without a second thought.

       

    Helsinki Cathedral

    Located on the Senate Square, Helsinki Cathedral is a neoclassical Lutheran church and a beautiful reminiscent of ancient Greek buildings. An impressive white landmark, the cathedral has become the symbol of the city with its white pillar, blue domes dotted with golden stars and statues of twelve apostles along the roof. Famous for its imposing architecture, Helsinki Cathedral is one of the top places to see in Finland.

       

    Finlandia Hall

    A concert and a convention hall designed by Alvar Aalto and built in 1971 lies on the shores of Toolo Bay. The stunning architectural marvel is famous for its acoustics. Finlandia Hall is located in the heart of the city and is one of the most prominent landmarks of Helsinki.

       

    Husky Safari

    A winter trip to the land of Santa is incomplete without the famous Husky Safari. A dog-sledging safari is one of the classic Lappish experiences, which takes you through Finland’s panoramas in the night.

       

    Hunting the Northern Lights

    A magical phenomenon, the Northern Lights are a sight to behold. It’s not easy to catch a glimpse of these surreal lights, but when you do, you’ll be speechless. Nature’s most spectacular show, Rovaniemi is the best place to hunt the Northern lights. But when the skies are clear and dark, depend on your luck and keep your eyes wide open.

       

    Crossing of the Arctic Circle

    Finland gives you a magical chance to cross the Arctic Circle on a snowmobile that runs through Santa Claus Village in Rovaniemi. A circular latitude which runs through the north of the Equator, Arctic Circle is the southernmost latitude from where the sun stays below or above the horizon for 24-hours. It’s undoubtedly an unforgettable experience in itself!

       

    Santa Claus Village

    Located in the capital city of Lapland in Finland, Santa Claus Village has some magical sights and attractions that offer an authentic Christmas experience. Well-known around the world, the village is considered as the hometown of Santa Claus and is built atop Arctic Circle. This is a once in a lifetime chance which will be etched to your memory forever. Before you leave, meet the jolly man himself and send a postcard from Santa Claus’s official post office. ...Read More

       

    Temppeliaukio Church

    Praised for its creativity and extraordinary use of natural space for a church, Temppeliaukio Church an architectural marvel of Helsinki. With glorious natural light glazing through the dome, the church is also used as a concert hall due to its acoustics created by rough and rugged rock surfaces of the interior. Widely known as Rock Church, the magnificent structure was established in 1969. One of the unique churches of the world, it is undoubtedly a must-visit place in Helsinki. ...Read More

       

    Sibelius Park

    Designed by Eila Hiltunen, Sibelius Park was constructed to honour Finnish composer Jean Sibelius. He was known for his set of seven symphonies and compositions such as Finlandia, the Karelia Suite and Valse Triste. He was one of the composers in the world who had candidly been able to capture the nature, history and myths of his own people in his notes. The Sibelius Monument is the most visited and loved attractions of Helsinki, which is made to look like a pipe organ. Take a tour of the park and gaze at this work of art at the monument, one of the well-known attractions of the city. ...Read More

       

    Suomenlinna Fortress

    Suomenlinna Sea Fortress is among the best Helsinki points of interest. A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the fortress is constructed on an island along with six nearby islands in Helsinki. With a great history and archaeology of the medieval military and defence of Finland, it is one of the major places to explore in the city.
