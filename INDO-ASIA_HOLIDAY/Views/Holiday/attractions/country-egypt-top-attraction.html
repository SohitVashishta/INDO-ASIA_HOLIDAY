    Top Tourist Places and Attractions in EGYPT

    Kom Ombo Temple

    Located in the town of Kom-Ombo, about 28 miles north of Aswan, the Temple, dating to the Ptolemy’s, is built on a high dune overlooking the Nile. The actual temple was started by Ptolemy VI Philometor in the early second century BC. Ptolemy XIII built the outer and inner hypostyle halls. The outer enclosure wall and part of the court were built by Augustus sometime after 30 BC, and are mostly gone. There are also tombs from the Old Kingdom in the vicinity of Kom-Ombo village. The Temple known as Kom Ombo is actually two temples consisting of a Temple to Sobek and a Temple of Haroeris. In ancient times, sacred crocodiles basked in the sun on the river bank near here. The Temple has scant remains, due first to the changing Nile, then the Copts who once used it as a church, and finally by builders who used the stones for new buildings. ...Read More

       

    Karnak Temple


    Karnak is the modern-day name for the ancient site of the Temple of Amun Ra at Thebes, and one of the must-visit places in Egypt. It was also called Pr-Imn, which means the House of Amon. The temple complex of Karnak is the most magnificent tourist spot in Luxor. It’s a delight to explore the colossal monuments of the Karnak Temple.

       

    Colossi of Memnon

    The famous gigantic statues carved out of hard yellowish-brown sandstone that represents Amenophis III seated on a cube-shaped throne, and once stood guard at the entrance to the king's temple, of which only scanty traces are left. The South Colossus is better preserved to the one in the north. The North Colossus is known for being the "musical statue” that brought flocks of visitors here during the Roman Imperial period. 

       

    Mortuary Temple of Hatshepsut

    The mortuary temple of Queen Hatshepsut is magnificently nestled at the foot of the sheer cliffs fringing the desert hills. The light-coloured sandstone of the temple looks beautiful against the yellow rocks behind. 

       

    Valley of the Kings

    Renowned as the world’s largest open-air museum, Luxor is known for the Valley of the Kings, an enormous valley that is hidden between rocky escarpments. Famous ancient kings such as Tutankhamun and Amenhotep were buried here. The final resting place for kings in 18, 19, 20th dynasties, their wonderful vivid wall paintings is the main attraction here. 

       

    Luxor Temple

    One of the most beautiful temples in Egypt built by Amenhotep III, Luxor Temple was known as Ipt-Rsyt in the New Kingdom, which means the southern shrine. The Temple run close and parallel to the river Nile from north to south, it was constructed on the site of a small Temple of Amon, built by kings of the 12th dynasty. 

       

    Temple of Horus at Edfu

    One of the most impressive and colossal structures of the Ptolemy Dynasty built in the 2nd Century B.C. Experience the vastness of the one of Egypt’s best-preserved temple. Located between the Egyptian cities Luxor and Aswan, the Temple of Horus is regarded as the second-largest temple in Egypt.

       

    Temple of Kom Ombo

    One of the Nile’s Valley most beautiful temples, Temple of Kom Ombo is known for its uniqueness. Dedicated to two gods, the entire temple holds two perfectly symmetrical sections. The left (side of the temple was dedicated to the god Haroeris and the right half to Sobek. An iconic spot in Egypt, the Temple of Kom Ombo is a must-visit.


       

    Unfinished Obelisk

    Nestled in Aswan’s Northern Quarry, the Unfinished Obelisk is an incredible part of Aswan’s heritage. It would have been the largest single standing stone in Egypt – had it not cracked in the middle. A great place to learn about Ancient Egypt in Aswan, it is undoubtedly one of the must-visit places!


       

    Philae Temple

    Dedicated to the Goddess Isis, the Philae Temple was constructed on an alluring island of Philae in the 7th century. The peaceful sanctum is adorned with vibrant inions, stone windows and beautifully lit hieroglyphics. Attending the Light and Sound Show here is among the best things to do in Aswan at night.

       

    Aswan High Dam

    Located near Aswan, Aswan High Dam is renowned around the world for being an engineering miracle when it was built in the 960s. An 11,811 feet long, 3215 feet thick at the base and 364 feet tall, the dam contain 18 times the material used in the Great Pyramid of Cheops.

       

    Khan el-Khalili Bazaar

    Renowned as one of the largest bazaars in the Middle East, Khan el-Khalili was originally founded as a watering stop for Caravanserai in the 14th Century. Now a chirpy, exciting and vibrant marketplace of Egypt, a stroll through the narrow streets filled with shops of spices, luxury fabrics and perfume is an experience in itself.

       

    Egyptian Museum

    Take a trip to the intriguing Egyptian Museum that houses the world’s largest collection of 120,000 ancient Egyptian artefacts featuring the renowned Tutankhamen collection with its magnificent gold death mask and sarcophagus and the royal Mummy room that has an additional eleven Pharaonic dignitaries.

       

    Memphis

    The capital of ancient Egypt, Memphis’s ruins are located near the town of Mit Rahina, south of Giza in Cairo. Memphis is said to be the cult centre of Ptah, creator God in ancient Egyptian religion. Only a few ruins remain of the great city, such as the Statue of Ramses II, and the Alabaster Sphinx. Saqqara was an ancient burial ground of Memphis for more than 3500 years and is one of the largest archaeological sites of Egypt.

       

    Great Sphinx of Giza

    One of the most amazing structures of Egypt’s marvellous collection of architecture, the Great Sphinx of Giza mesmerises travellers with its mightiness! Carved out of a single block, it is said to be one of the oldest and largest known structure in the ancient world. The word "sphinx", which means 'strangler', was first given by the Greeks to a fabulous mythical creature that had the head of a man and the body of a lion Of Egypt. The head and face of the Sphinx certainly reflect a style that belongs to Egypt's Old Kingdom, majorly to the 4th dynasty. The limestone Sphinx structure is part of the main complex of the Great Pyramids.

    Great Pyramids

    Constructed around 4500 years ago, the Great Pyramids are the monumental tombs that showcase the glimpses of Egypt’s Old Kingdom. The Great Pyramid of Giza is the main part of a complex setting of buildings that included two mortuary temples in honour of Khufu (one close to the pyramid and one near the Nile), three smaller pyramids for Khufu's wives, an even smaller pyramid, a raised causeway connecting the two temples, and small Mastaba tombs surrounding the pyramid for nobles. Take a plunge into the mysteries of pharaohs, their beliefs in the afterlife, and the intriguing architecture of one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.
