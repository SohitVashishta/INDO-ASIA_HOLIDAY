    Top Tourist Places and Attractions in NEPAL

    Chitwan National Park

    A UNESCO world heritage site, Chitwan National Park is one of the most iconic national parks of Nepal. Covering 932 square kilometers of grassland, marshland and forests, the park has a decent wildlife population and thus, spotting animals and birds is quite easy and exciting. If you get lucky, you would spot Royal Bengal Tiger, the famous leopards of the region or sloth bear!

    ...Read More

       

    Devi’s Falls

    Davi's Falls is an extraordinary waterfall just about 2 km away from the Pokhara airport. A beautiful landmark of Pokhara, Devi's waterfall is a must-visit in Nepal. Witness the beauty of nature as you see this colossal fall struggling with rocks to set itself free.

    ...Read More

       

    Bindabasini Temple

    The ancient Bindhyabasini Temple in Pokhara is settled on top of a small hill. The temple is dedicated to Goddess Durga and is one of the most visited temples in Pokhara.> 

    ...Read More

       

    Tal Barahi Temple

    The temple is settled in the vicinity of the Phewa Lake. Beautifully nestled on the lake, the deities of the temple are religiously worshipped and are one of the prominent pilgrimages of Nepal. Dedicated to Goddess Varahi, an incarnation of Goddess Durga, both Hindu and Buddhist followers visit the temple to worship.

    ...Read More

       

    World Peace Pagoda

    Shanti Stupa in Pokhara also called as the World Peace Pagoda is a stunning monument located on the Anadu Hill overlooking the majestic Phewa Lake with the charming Annapurna Mountain range in the background. It is a striking landmark and one of the most popular places to visit in Pokhara. There are many ways to reach this stupa like hiking, boating, and walking through a jungle. Learn about the brilliant pagoda architecture and indulge yourself to the Buddhist teachings and ures.

    ...Read More

       

    Phewa Lake

    A beautiful freshwater lake, Phewa Lake is one of the most visited spots in Pokhara. It is the second-largest lake in Nepal with many exciting activities like canoeing, boating, swimming, sailing, fishing, kayaking and bird watching.

    ...Read More

       

    Pokhara

    Nestled amidst the Annapurna Himalayan Range, Pokhara Valley is the second-largest valley and is one of the best places to visit in Nepal. Situated on the shoreline of a peaceful Lake Phewa, the city is embedded with beautiful natural landscapes, such as the snow-capped Annapurna Massif and the surrounding Himalayan range. A hotspot for adventure lovers, Pokhara is a picture-perfect place to spend your days in Nepal. 

    ...Read More

       

    Nagarkot

    Located close to the Kathmandu Valley, Nagarkot is among the popular places to visit in Nepal. It is known for its mesmerizing Himalayan views including the Mount Everest on the North-Eastern side. It is one of the best places to enjoy the majestic sunrise over the Himalayas in Nepal. Renowned for its natural beauty, Nagarkot is just a short drive away from Kathmandu. 

    ...Read More

       

    Potters' Square

    A centre of Bhaktapur’s ceramic industry, and a fascinating place to wander around to explore the authentic Newari pottery kilns, open-air drying areas and clay pottery!"> 

    ...Read More

       

    Dattatreya Square

    The square is named after the Dattatreya temple, where Dattatreya, a part of the trinity is worshipped. The eye-catching Dattatreya Temple was originally built in 1427, supposedly using the timber from a single tree. The elegant square is one of the must-visit places in Bhaktapur. 

    ...Read More

       

    Taumadhi Square

    Located right next to the famous Durbar Square, Taumadhi Square is a must-visit place to know about the ancient legends and adore ancient architecture. The beautifully structured stones and carvings on the wood are a highlight of the square. Nyatapola temple, Til Madhav Narayan Temple, and Bisket Yatra are some of the must explore places in the square.

    ...Read More

       

    Bhaktapur

    One of three medieval cities in the Kathmandu Valley was historically described as the best-preserved city-state in the valley. The city is divided into three squares, each of them filled with temples and other religious structures. A place for devotees, the temples, palaces and shrines are a must-visit here.

    ...Read More

       

    Changu Narayan Temple

    Located in the Bhaktapur district, Changu Narayan is the oldest temple in Nepal and was built in authentic Newari style architecture. With its two-storey brick-red edifice, the elegant temple is settled atop a hill which is also called Changu or sometimes Dolagiri. Dedicated to Lord Vishnu, it is one of the most iconic temples to visit in Bhaktapur. 

    ...Read More

       

    Patan Durbar Square

    Patan is Nepal’s third-largest city and is also known as Lalitpur. Durbar Square is one of the prime attractions in the city, locally called the Patan Museum. A UNESCO World Heritage site, Patan Durbar Square houses several Buddhist and Hindu temples, shrines and historic structures with a unique blend of the predominant Nepali religions and brilliant Newari architecture.

    ...Read More

       

    Pashupatinath Temple

    One of the oldest and most sacred temples in Nepal, Pashupatinath Temple is located along the River Bagmati in Kathmandu and is dedicated to the deity of Nepal, Lord Pashupatinath, also known as Lord Shiva. One of the UNESCO world heritage sites, this temple in Nepal is a collection of different ashrams, temples, and inions which have been there for a long period. It is the most renowned and must-visit tourist places in Nepal.

    ...Read More

       

    Boudhanath Stupa

    Set against the beautiful landscape of the Himalayas, the colossal white Boudhnath Stupa was built around 600 AD and is one of the most iconic Buddhist stupas in Nepal. Popularly known as the Khasa Chaitya, the highlight of the temple is the magnificent Stupa. One of the UNESCO world heritage sites, Boudhnath Temple is one of the best places to visit in Nepal.

    ...Read More

       

    Swayambhunath Stupa (Monkey Temple)

    A UNESCO world heritage site, Swayambhunath Stupa is said to be the oldest Buddhist stupa in Nepal. Surrounded by monkeys and soaring above the city on a lofty hilltop, the ‘Monkey Temple’ is a fascinating jumble of Buddhist and Hindu traditions. The temple is a popular spot for people who follow Buddhism and is also amongst the most beautiful tourist attractions in Nepal.

    ...Read More

       

    Kathmandu Durbar Square

    One of the most visited sites in Kathmandu, Kathmandu Durbar Square is a UNESCO world heritage site. The place has witnessed and received many kings who ruled over Nepal for a long time. One of the most remarkable legacies of Kathmandu’s traditional architecture, Kathmandu Durbar Square is one of the must-see places in the city. It is also a prime spot for cultural programmes in Nepal. Head there during the evening to experience the best of Nepal’s culture and cuisine.

    ...Read More
