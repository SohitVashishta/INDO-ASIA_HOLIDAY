    Top Tourist Places and Attractions in MALAYSIA

    Underwater World

    One of the Langkawi’s best-known spots, Underwater World is one of the largest aquariums in Malaysia. Underwater World serves a purpose of entertainment, knowledge and education. It is built to grow awareness on the importance of conversing our marine and aquatic life. The gigantic structure is home to more than 200 different species of marine and freshwater fish including harbour seals, rockhopper penguins, sharks, giant rays and the enormous Amazonian arapaima – the biggest freshwater fish in the world. ...Read More

       

    Sunway Lagoon

    The Sunway Lagoon is an epitome of fun! With water rides that whirls and swirls, surf beach, 360-degree revolving pirate ship, Sunway Lagoon is the perfect place to spend your day relaxing. Indulge in adventure, explore park rides, interactive games, wave and surfs pools and even a zoo.

       

    Sultan Abdul Samad Building

    The Sultan Abdul Samad Building is a late nineteenth century building located along Jalan Raja in front of the Dataran Merdeka and the Royal Selangor Club in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia. The building originally housed the offices of the British colonial administration, and was known simply as Government Offices in its early years. In 1974 it was renamed after Sultan Abdul Samad, the reigning sultan of Selangor at the time when construction began.

       

    Snake Temple

    Built-in 1850 to honour the famous Buddhist priest and healer Chor Soo Kong, the beautiful temple holds an interesting history to it. It is said that the monk gave shelter to snakes after the temple was built and hence it was called The Snake Temple. The major tourist attraction in Malaysia, one can spot a number of snakes in the temple which is a unique sight.

       

    Petronas Twin Towers

    World’s famous and architectural marvels, Petronas Twin Towers is the crown of Kuala Lumpur, nestled in the heart of the city. The tallest twin towers in the world will amaze you with its magnificent structures and amazing architecture. The breath-taking skyline from the towers is one of the must-see views in Malaysia. The fascinating towers are linked through a sky bridge at the 41st and 42nd floor to allow people to travel within the tower. The iconic 88 storey chrome and steel towers are one of the must-visit spots in Malaysia. ...Read More

       

    National Palace

    Nestled around the breath-taking landscapes and verdant nature, the National Palace or the Jalan Istana of Malaysia is one of the most admirable sights. Open to all, National Palace has a separate part inside where the Royal Museum exhibits the paintings of national artist. The simple yet elegant palace is a must-visit place in Kuala Lumpur.

       

    National Monument

    The National Monument is a beautiful sculpture built in the remembrance of the brave soldiers who died fighting for the independence of the country. Located around the Lake Gardens, the National Monument is a must visit in Kuala Lumpur.

       

    Fishing Village

    A bustling fishing village of Penang is a different world altogether. The quaint fishing villages and the alluring lakes are one of the best ways for the ardent fisherman to indulge in a hobby! Teluk Bahang, located on the northwest of Penang is a beautiful village with a mesmerising Butterfly Farm. Explore the distinct cultures and traditional of a small community in the fishing village and know more about the history of the place.

       

    Eagle Square

    Eagle Square, also known as Dataran Lang, is one of the most visited attractions in Langkawi. Nestled 12 meters above the star-shaped concrete deck overlooking the Kuah Jetty, the iconic Eagle statue welcomes the traveller with its wings wide open. Located around beautiful surroundings with an alluring mountain view, and small ponds, it is one of the most-visited places in Langkawi.

       

    Cenang Beach

    One of the most beautiful beaches in Malaysia, Cenang Beach is a paradise amidst palms and coconut trees. With thrilling water sports and mesmerising vistas, Cenang Beach is one of the must-visit beaches in Langkawi. The bustling beach offers the best experience of nightlife in Malaysia.

       

    Batik Factory

    Batik Factory is one of the largest handmade batik producers in the country. Witness the intriguing batik-making process that has been going on from the 4th century. Built in 1973 by Craft Batik Sdn Bhd, the factory is set in Teluk Bahang, few minutes’ walk from the Penang Botanic Gardens. Amidst the verdant tropical surroundings, The Batik Factory is a must-visit spot in Penang, Malaysia.
